How To Read k And Accuracy
==========================
k is the number of features kept after ANOVA ranking within a chosen feature subset.

Examples
--------
- k=100: keep the top 100 ranked features from that subset.
- k=50: keep the top 50 ranked features.
- k=all: keep every usable feature in that subset; no ANOVA pruning.

Going forward, the primary metric is raw accuracy, not balanced accuracy.
Balanced accuracy is still retained in the detailed CSV-backed source files for reference, but the report tables in this folder are written around raw accuracy because that matches the benchmark text tables.
