Bonferroni Saliency vs Permutation Saliency
==========================================
Method
------
Both saliency variants use the same Phase 1 language-specific best `all_universal` model per language.
The only change is the feature list used to build the AD-oriented burden radar plots.
- Permutation map: top local model features from held-out permutation importance.
- Bonferroni map: top language-specific Bonferroni-significant features ranked by p-value.

English
-------
Permutation: n=16 | mean burden gap=11.9 | Cohen's d=1.09 | strongest group=len (19.1)
Bonferroni: n=16 | mean burden gap=14.8 | Cohen's d=1.06 | strongest group=len (19.1)
Overlap with permutation feature set: 8/16 | shared: syn_mean_dependency_length, len_utterance_count, syn_utterance_count, syn_std_dependency_length, syn_num_ratio, syn_subordination_ratio, syn_clause_density, graph_density
Clearer by mean-burden separation: Bonferroni
Why it looks clearer:
Bonferroni features are more consistently AD-vs-HC separated one by one, so the radar shapes tend to open up more cleanly between classes.

Overall
-------
- English: Bonferroni
- Chinese: Permutation
- Greek: Bonferroni
- Spanish: Permutation
- Bonferroni maps are usually easier to justify as biomarker-style plots because every plotted feature passed a corrected univariate test.
- Permutation maps remain better if the goal is fidelity to what the fitted classifier actually used.
